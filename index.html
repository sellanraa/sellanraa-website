who and where i am

My name is Connor Bell (b. 1979 in Oshkosh, WI USA). Right now I’m living in Beechmont, a beautiful neighborhood in south Louisville with two wonderful little dachshunds that bring so much joy to my life. I’m a passionate person and spend my time pursuing knowledge and creativity. My curiosity feels insatiable and being creative, primarily through music, is an outlet for all that can’t be expressed so easily in life. After all, words are only one way to communicate.

I’ll keep an archive of these little essays linked with my writing samples. I didn’t really intend this to be a blog, and it’s certainly not a regular update, but I do like that I can trace a bit of a line by looking these over.

25.05.07: In the interest of trying to expand my writing samples, I’ve added to the links in the sidebar here (or below on mobile). One sample I decided to include was a summary of the first half of 2024, which was an emotional rollercoaster of frustration. There’s a lot more emotion in that essay, but it felt very good and important to write things out as a way to process that emotion. If you want a window into the ride of life while unemployed, hop to that one.

Life remains stressful while I try to keep searching for the new normal. I try to compartmentalize and I’m grateful for so much support and the foundation I can lean on with my dwindling 401k, potential to rent out a room in the house, and unemployment insurance, but I don’t obviously want to rely on those things.

Outside of looking for work, this is the season of gardening. I also decided to let my MLB streaming package lapse for the first time in at least 15 years I’d say. Bob Uecker passing and money increasingly twisting the league in weird ways just meant I wasn’t as inspired. I miss Gary, Keith, and Ron in the SNY booth calling Mets games though. I felt I could rationalize subscribing to the Criterion Channel, as a result, and it sure is nice watching some classics I never scoped and new thoughtful and thought-provoking movies.

Be well in the weird times.

    Sellanraa is the name of Isak’s farm in Knut Hamsun’s “Growth of the Soil”. The story of Isak and his farm have become something of a guidepost in my life.

    Of a patient, simple, and steady approach. Of accepting the uncontrollable. Of not being distracted by endless comparisons or thinking life is a race. Of embracing the process.
