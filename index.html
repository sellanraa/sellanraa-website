who and where i am

My name is Connor Joseph Bell (b. 1979 in Oshkosh, WI USA). Right now I’m living in Beechmont, a beautiful neighborhood in south Louisville with two wonderful little dachshunds that bring so much joy to my life. I’m a passionate person and spend my time pursuing knowledge and creativity. My curiosity feels insatiable and being creative, primarily through music, is an outlet for all that can’t be expressed so easily in life. After all, words are only one way to express oneself.

23.05.16:

I’m here. Don't expect these missives very regularly.

Back in March, I had to put Tink to sleep after what the vets think was a heart attack. It was rough to go through, but I’m thankful that there was no questioning whether the time was ‘right’. The silence in the weeks that followed was pretty glaring. It quickly became evident how much of my day was occupied with ensuring her every need was met. I knew I wanted a second dog quickly so I adopted a malnourished piebald mini (micro?) from a rescue called Kentucky Hopeful Paws about three weeks after Tink passed. The new pup was supposed to be skittish, so I had intended to name her Freydis, but I didn’t really see that at all so decided to name her Dagny, which is supposedly Norwegian for ‘new day’. I figured a break from an old ween would be good for my health even though I really treasure an old pooch. Soon enough Ruby will be that old lady and before that time comes, she can teach Dagny the ways of Clan Murph.

Otherwise, I’ve largely been living a normal life where I haven’t even felt guilty about not working on music. Instead, my days (outside of work) are filled with walking more with the dogs, kettlebell workouts, and watching movies (had a heck of a stretch of yakuza movies!) and hockey/baseball. Musically, I’ve mostly focused on a three piece with my friends Ben Vandermeer and Joey Yates. About fifteen years ago, we had a band very briefly with the silly working title of Party Box, and we’ve revived some of those never completed song ideas and have written new ones. It’s slow going as we have ambitious ideas for what we’re trying to accomplish.

Work has been challenging. People often ask me how it’s going and I don’t know how to answer. It’s overwhelming to be seven months in and still not have a sense of how you’re doing. I have loved the challenge and love learning so much every day, and yet, I never feel like it’s enough or that I’m acquiring the skills quickly enough. I’m grateful that I’m surrounded by a patient and supportive team. I just desperately want to feel like I'm contributing and it's frustrating knowing that I'm not where I want to be. Trying to remember to be patient and to trust the process.

I’m going to keep an archive of these little essays linked with my writing samples. I didn’t really intend this to be a blog, and it’s certainly not a regular update, but I do like that I can trace a bit of a line by looking these over.
