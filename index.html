who and where i am

My name is Connor Bell (b. 1979 in Oshkosh, WI USA). Right now I’m living in Beechmont, a beautiful neighborhood in south Louisville with two wonderful little dachshunds that bring so much joy to my life. I’m a passionate person and spend my time pursuing knowledge and creativity. My curiosity feels insatiable and being creative, primarily through music, is an outlet for all that can’t be expressed so easily in life. After all, words are only one way to communicate.

I’ll keep an archive of these little essays linked with my writing samples. I didn’t really intend this to be a blog, and it’s certainly not a regular update, but I do like that I can trace a bit of a line by looking these over.

25.08.13:

Summer in Kentucky. Insufferable. Otherwise, I’ve been in a good rhythm and after a quiet month or so, the interviews have picked up again. Those glimmers of hope make a huge difference to my energy levels. I have enjoyed the opportunity to grow through all this tumult and almost always feel good coming out of interviews, so I reckon it’s only a matter of time. It’s been a cool opportunity to connect with and get to know so many different people and I try to focus on that human connection that is lacking in so many ways in our modern reality. LinkedIn has been a daily thing to interface with too. Despite how gross it feels in many ways, I’ve tried to use it in an authentic way, which feels counter to the lowest common denominator ‘hot takes’ that predominate there. More importantly, it is super cool to reconnect with former students there. I see so many more but don’t want to come across as the old weirdo, so let them initiate contact. When we do connect, it’s neat to see how they’ve continued on their path in life after leaving school.

Otherwise, I’ve stayed pretty quiet. I pushed my running a bit harder, even hitting 6.2 miles on a run, but my heel pain immediately reminded me why I should be content doing 3 5ks a week and leaving it at around that. Music remains a balm and I continue plowing through a slew of amazing movies on the Criterion Channel. The Brewers are crushing it and I recently caved when the MLB package was on sale, so streaming some games again has been nice.

I turn 46 soon enough, which is an opportunity to reflect, grow, and consider your own mortality. This is further reinforced by my uncle passing away last week. My family here in Louisville has always been troubling and it’s been an opportunity to ponder how I can be a better uncle than I experienced growing up as well as navigating the complex feelings of generational ghosts that haunt us all. Aside from my mom’s cousin, who was so close I consider her like an aunt (and her husband like an uncle), I have no family left in Louisville. I was in such a rush to escape and being in Madison as an 18 year old was a reminder of what I didn’t recognize was worth loving about Louisville. Coming home soon after to finish school here was a lifetime ago. It’s wild to think I’ve basically been here ever since despite feeling like that weird northerner more often than not. I love Louisville but the feelings I have for it are hard to encapsulate and I often think leaving is long overdue. Maybe an employment opportunity will make that inevitable. Housing costs being what they are, staying put makes a lot of sense too.

I don’t have too much to say but it’s been a few months, so I figured an update was in order, particularly given the giant list of movies I’m adding below.

Be well in the weird times.
