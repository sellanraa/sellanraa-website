who and where i am

My name is Connor Joseph Bell (b. 1979 in Oshkosh, WI USA). Right now I’m living in Beechmont, a beautiful neighborhood in south Louisville with two wonderful little dachshunds that bring so much joy to my life. I’m a passionate person and spend my time pursuing knowledge and creativity. My curiosity feels insatiable and being creative, primarily through music, is an outlet for all that can’t be expressed so easily in life. After all, words are only one way to express oneself.

23.10.11:

I don’t talk about my musical pursuits much outside the weirdo musician bubble, but it feels right to mention here that it’s a huge passion of mine. For as long as I can remember, I’ve played music. Initially, I was forced to play classical piano as a child and it was a chore. But in middle school I discovered punk rock and felt empowered. No longer was it about virtuosity, it was about freedom and expression. I was intrinsically motivated ever since and thankfully had a solid musical foundation already from those years of piano. Music is ultimately deeply therapeutic, and even when working alone it fosters connection with others. When playing in a band, the lows are lower and the highs are higher as the creative spark becomes downright magical. I always keep my own music going to satisfy the compulsion when collaboration isn’t possible.

I say all this to introduce a side of who I am that isn’t necessarily traditionally recognized in professional/career settings, and yet the skills I’ve cultivated over a lifetime of music are impossible to calculate.

I say all this now to mention that I’m playing my first solo shows in four years in October. I’ve only played two shows in that span of time, both collaborations. It’s strange having gone so long with so little live performance, but between personal and global tumult, it just wasn’t a priority. Nevertheless, I’ve continued to work though because whether I’m sharing it or not, it’s still a central practice in my life. I’ve sacrificed a lot to continue to follow the muse and I wouldn’t have it any other way. I do it because I have to.
