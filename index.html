who and where i am

My name is Connor Bell (b. 1979 in Oshkosh, WI USA). Right now I’m living in Beechmont, a beautiful neighborhood in south Louisville with two wonderful little dachshunds that bring so much joy to my life. I’m a passionate person and spend my time pursuing knowledge and creativity. My curiosity feels insatiable and being creative, primarily through music, is an outlet for all that can’t be expressed so easily in life. After all, words are only one way to communicate.

I’ll keep an archive of these little essays linked with my writing samples. I didn’t really intend this to be a blog, and it’s certainly not a regular update, but I do like that I can trace a bit of a line by looking these over.

26.03.26:
Things are quiet here and I’ve settled into the new normal of unemployment. Exercise, job hunt, reading, creative time, dog snuggles, and intermittent spring yard prep and gardening. I’m pretty thankful to continue to be able to be deliberate in making my next career move. Life is good.

I should note with my spring reading below, I’ve been navigating “A Thousand Plateaus” and supplementing that challenging book with numerous other books that I occasionally reference to help me understand what is quite a daunting task. It’s been a really great challenge, but I’m sad I’ve not read any fiction all year. I’ve taken breaks between chapters to rest my brain and read some other non-fiction though.

    Sellanraa is the name of Isak’s farm in Knut Hamsun’s “Growth of the Soil”. The story of Isak settling down and patiently building his farmstead have become something of a guidepost in my life.

    Of a patient, simple, and steady approach. Of accepting the uncontrollable. Of not being distracted by endless comparisons or thinking life is a race. Of embracing the process.
